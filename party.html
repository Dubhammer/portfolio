Balancing act between needs and wants of stakeholders and users.

Proposal

A well-known party venue wanted a concept of MVP (Minimum Viable Product) party booking system. They had number of specific needs and wants based on their own research but open to receive more feedback. I was tasked with addtional research and design solution to improve the user experience within limited time span.

Solution

After going through research, including competition analysis, a new booking system was proposed. It implemented all the requirements by focusing on the following points that helped me deliver the prototype that "really hit the mark" .

  • Naturally acquiring information through logical flow and layout
  • Improving requested features through additional research
  • Staying within the scope of MVP and avoid feature bloat

Channleges

Based on provided interview data with target demographics groups, the request focused on providing missing context and options to aid informed decision-making process for users. The scope of the project, limited time frame, and technical limitations of scheduling and payment solutions vendor had to be considered for the following feature sets.

Collect contact information as the first step of the experience
VIP guest information for personalization
Persistent way to access FAQ and Live Chat agent
Add-on customization with filters
Detailed itemized list during checkout
Offer discount on future parties when singing up for membership

Research

To better understand the general business practices within the industry, the booking process of the direct competitors and adjacent venues were analyzed in perspective of intended target demographics: parents, and guardians of the children. The analysis has revealed number of key areas that could be improved for more frictionless user experience.

Design

The focus of the design concept was implementing requested features augmented by consistency, clear hierarchy, and simplified brand identity. In addition, fragmented information on the website was collected and presented in contextually relevant manner to promote informed decision-making process. The mobile design was chosen based on client research and ease of accessing the service for busy parents.

1. Tracking & Assistance

  • Persistent 15 minutes reservation timer per session

  • Tracker for displaying booking progress

  • Floating Action Button for accessing customer support

2. Flow & Hierarchy

  • Put emphasis on logical flow of information agumented with user needs

  • Focus on streamlining the process instead of oversimplifiying

  • Present additional or relevant information after user choice

3. Familiar Design

  • Use familiar design solution for aiding ease of use

  • Help users focus on the content and checkout process

4. User Choice

  • Faciliate informed decision making by providing right amount of information

  • Provide filtering option when applicable

5. Provide Context

  • Help user by providing situational and emotional context

  • Provide fully detailed summary of user choices

  • Group and categorize information to help users with inherent complexity

6. Calculated Friction

  • Some user frustrations are necessary for providing proper functions and accurate information

7. Peak End & Follow Up

  • Provide sense of accomplishment for finishing a task

  • Provide notifications for reminder and tools for user control

Reflections

Always be ready to learn and grow
  • This project was very spontaneous, and I had to perform large number of tasks in short timeframe. Instead of being stuck on a part of the problem, I had to learn to see the bigger picture and deliver the result that satisfied both internal and external stakeholders.
Justify your decisions through research
  • You need to be able to justify your decisions. Additional research I’ve done has helped me understand the pain points of users during checkout process and UX laws helped me pivot the focus to balancing the needs of stakeholder and users.
Somethings are beyond your control
  • Don’t get discouraged by unsatisfactory result that is beyond your control. Instead of dwelling in what could have been, focus on how you can use this learning experience for future opportunities. Sometimes you just have to go with the flow.
