Building confidence and independence through respectful, enjoyable, and memorable activities.

Problem

Helping friends and family with learning technology can be a challenging task. People providing assistance may feel undervalued and exploited and people receiving help may feel lost and frustrated. It starts with good intentions, but it could quickly devolve into a major relationship issue with lasting consequences. How can we create a respectful, enjoyable, and memorable experience that benefits everyone involved?

Solution

Based on research, heavy reliance on family members and acquaintances is due to insecurity caused by low confidence level. The approach to the issue shifted from eliminating knowledge gap to providing emotional fulfillment and building confidence to be more independent.

  • Co-creating manual kit that give agency to learners through co-creation activities
  • Help retain knowledge through personalized procedure-oriented learning process
  • Make the teaching and learning experience part of bonding experience to strengthen relationship

Challenges

People can encounter significant challenges while trying to provide aid to other people. I have personally faced multitude of issues when trying to improve technological literacy of my parents. I was frustrated but remembering conversations I have had with other people in similar situation made me realize that there needs to be a fundamental shift in how we approach this problem. I wanted to utilize my skills to find a solution that can help solve communication issues for people.

Design

Based on insights gathered from research, the design focuses on resolving communication issue, providing emotional fulfillment, and building confidence to be more independent. The design process involved collaboration with my mother who agreed to evaluate different workflow to find optimal learning process for herself and further discover broader guiding principles to assist other people.

Session 1

This was a trial run to see feasibility of co-creation activities. Making online appointments were always my responsibility but I convinced my mother to try making an appointment on her own while I was available as guide to answer questions. There were some resistances, but I was firm on not doing the task for her. The result was very encouraging since she realized her own capabilities of completing the task with minimal guidance.

Session 2

This was more proper attempt at co-creating manuals with my mother. Together, we gathered number of different notebooks and arts and crafts supplies. She evaluated different options and chose notebook and pen she liked. She questions why she couldn't just use built in memo app for record keeping but she realized it was not optimal due to many restrictions.

A

Words alone can't help her understand the procedure correctly.

B

Screenshots may contain too many irreverent information.

C

Memo app has limited room for flexibility or creativity.

D

Limited split screen function for side by side comparison.

I have asked her to think of topics she may need help in between sessions.She wanted to know how to send multiple photos on messaging app KaKaoTalk and try out text to speech feature for responding to messages. She seemed more engaged with manual co-creation activity because she being part of decision making process.

Session 3

This session focused on refining record keeping process and trying to add more creativity into the mix. Her goal was learning split screen function on her tablet and different method of using translation app. She was especially motivated to learn translation functions since it would be useful for quickly translating conversation to gain context of the situation.

She was satisfied with how things were progressing but wanted to better organize her notebook. We were thinking of different options, and she suggested using page marking tapes to colour code different activities. She suggested future version could have dedicated sections and page numbers for more refined grouping and categorization of information.

The Result
  • She was genuinely delight about manual co-creation experience.
  • She loved her own explanation of process for better understanding.
  • She was actively thinking of things she wanted to learn.

Reflections

Reflect upon your actions to make improvement
  • For years, I have had a trouble making positive impact while helping my parents learn new concepts and procedures. I realized my attitude and lack of clear communication was a major contributing factor. I had to fundamentally shift my thinking and approach to make a desired impact and improve existing relationship.
Don't underestimate the complexities of realizing a simple idea
  • The idea started with creating memorable learning experiences for seniors but there were many things to consider. Everyone has different relationship dynamics, knowledge base, commitment, and emotional intelligence which could result in vastly different quality of experiences. You should never underestimate the scope and commitment required to execute your simple idea.
